Fried Cabbage

This is a very flavorful cabbage dish that has BACON in it and is very simple and easy to make!

"This is one of those dishes that should be made and consumed on the same day as it doesn't do very well as leftovers! If you have a cast iron skillet large enough (12" or larger), use it to prepare this recipe! P.S. There's bacon in this!!"

Ingredients:

  • 6 - 8 strips of bacon (more if you like, but don't over do it) Hahaha!
  • 1 head red or green cabbage - sliced (note: red cabbage is a tad sweeter and makes the dish more interesting)
  • 1 diced onion (I prefer yellow or red, again for the more complex flavors.)
  • 1/4 cup chicken broth (canned is fine, but if you have some homemade left over from a roast chicken, even better)
  • 1 tsp good cider vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp to 1 tsp fresh ground pepper

Instructions:

  1. Fry bacon in cast iron skillet until crispy. (Do not sample unless you made more than you need.)
  2. Drain Bacon, crumble and set aside.
  3. Fry onion until golden, then place cabbage into skillet on medium heat. Cook until cabbage is wilted.
  4. Sprinkle whatever crumbled bacon is left into the skillet. Add the chicken stock.
  5. Simmer for 10 minutes (no lid please!)
  6. Serve with a nice India Pale Ale!
