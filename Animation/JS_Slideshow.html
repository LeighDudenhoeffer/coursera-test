  • HERBS

    A collection of perennial herbs.

  • Lavender

    A stand of beautiful stand of blooming lavender. This aromatic herb can be started from a propler cutting and root hormone.

  • Sweet Marjoram

    A stand of luscious sweet marjoram. This is very easy to grow and easy to keep over the winter indoors. Add this to your pot roast!

  • Parsley

    Parsley ready for harvesting. Pinch it off, don't use a scissors. Use it as a garnish or add it to a good stirfry!

  • Peppermint

    This is a very forgiving and versatile herb. Use it for tea, candy making, garnishes, or dry it.

  • Rosemary

    This is another favorite that over winters very well indoors. Use it in meat dishes, like pork chops or roasts.

  • Sage

    Sage doesn't like to be inside. Let this grow year round in temperate zones 7 - 8. Cover with leaves and mulch and wrap in burlap and black plastic for zones 3 - 6.

  • Sweet Basil

    Basil is one of those herbs that drys well, but tastes best when fresh. Make a pesto, add it to your Bloody Mary or tomato soup. It winters well indoors.

  • Thyme

    Thyme is really awesome paired with a good dark chocolate or added to hot cocoa while it's simmering. Put it in all of your soups and stews.

  • Yarrow

    Yarrow is a very interesting herb that grows best in the wild. This is a medicinal herb that lowers a fever and can be used as a tincture in a small spray bottle to apply to wounds for quick healing.
